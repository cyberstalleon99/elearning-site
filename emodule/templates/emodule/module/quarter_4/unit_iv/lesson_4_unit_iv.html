How to Measure your Own Rib Length

Determine the diameter of the base, 8" in the illustration. Add to that the height you want, 4" in the illustration and then add the 6" for tucking in: 8" + 8" (4 for each side) + 6" = 22". The number of stakes to be cut will be determined by the size reed you use and how close together you want the spokes. You can use more stakes and start weaving closer in to the center if you taper the center as in the illustration.

Suggested Basketry Projects

Baskets are made with standard shapes and sizes. Some baskets are unique to various parts of the world. Most of them look very much alike as if they were machine made but indeed, they are explicitly handmade.

FASHIONABLE WEAVE SCHOOL BAG

I. Objectives:

  1. Make a fashionable weave school bag.
  2. Develop industry and love for work.
  3. Apply one's aesthetic sense in making fashionable weave school bag.
  4. Show enthusiasm by working.

II. Design Drawing/Illustration:

III. Tools and Materials:

Tools:

  • Scissors
  • Ruler
  • Tape Measure
  • Pins
  • Needle and thread

Materials:

Quantity Unit Description of Materials
32 pieces 30-inch long, 1/2-inch wide Plastic Strap (2 colors)

IV. Procedure:

  1. Take one piece of plastic strap and place it vertically across a flat surface. Place the second piece horizontally across the first. Line up the center pins.
  2. Lay another 15 vertical pieces alternately over and under the horizontal pieces. The pin in the horizontal piece should be kept centered. Pin the vertical pieces to the horizontal piece.
  3. Weave another 15 horizontal pieces alternately over and under the vertical pieces. The pin in the first vertical piece should be kept centered as shown.
  4. Pin the pieces at the four right-angled corners to secure them. The corners of the basket will be formed at the center of each set of six pieces, indicated by the arrow.
  5. Pick up the side of the basket farthest away from you and with three pieces in each hand, make a corner by bending the pieces toward each other at right angle.
  6. Weave each of the six pieces over and under once. Secure the pieces with pins. Rotate the basket and create the other three corners in the same way. Pin to secure.
  7. Continue to weave over one, under one toward the top of the basket, one row at a time until you reach the desired height. Wherever necessary, pin the pieces to hold them in place.
  8. To make a saw-toothed top edge of the bag, begin doubling the pieces back on one another as shown in the illustration. The loose ends will hang out until trimmed.
  9. To complete each saw-toothed edge at the top of the basket (begun as illustrated in step 8), make the fold shown here. Do these all the way round the top of the bag.
  10. Work anti-clockwise around, carefully repeating the motions of steps 8 and 9 until all the plastic strap pieces have been folded and pinned in place.
  11. Continue to work anti-clockwise. Take each loose end formed in steps 8 and 9 and slide it under the next plastic strap piece below it. Pull the ends all the way through.
  12. Cut off all the ends facing one way, then those facing the other way. Before cutting, pull ends of ribbon slightly, so that when cut, the ends will slide back and be hidden.
  13. Push back the pieces that cross the hidden ends, and with the needle and thread, tack each end with an overhand stitch. Remove all the remaining pins.
  14. To make a braid handle, measure the length that you want for your handle, add one-third of that length to your measurement. Then, double the total to find out your "cutting length."
    Example:
    • Cutting Length = [Handle Length + 1/3(Handle Length)] x 2
    • Length of handle = 18 inches
    • Add one-third = 24 inches
    • Double the total = 48 inches = cutting length
  15. Cut your lengths as per measurements-the more lengths you use, the thicker your handle will be.
  16. Once you have all lengths cut, fold lengths in half and tie a loose knot in the folded end to hold them together.
  17. Separate your colors into groups (or mix depending on the look you want).
  18. Hold the knot as you need a little tension, and then bring the left-hand strand over the middle strand to the center.
  19. Bring the right-hand strand over the new middle strand to the center.
  20. Alternate bringing the outermost strand to the center, then stop when you have about 6 inches left. Attach the braid handle to the bag.

MARKET BASKET

I. Objectives:

  1. Make a market basket.
  2. Develop industry and love for work.
  3. Apply one's aesthetic sense in making market basket.
  4. Show enthusiasm by working.
  5. Show creativity in promoting the product.

II. Design Drawing/Illustration:

III. Tools and Materials:

Tools:

  • Sharp Scissors
  • Sack Needle
  • Awl
  • Pin for marking

Materials:

Quantity Unit Description of Materials
Dried raffia leaves or water hyacinth

IV. Procedure:

  1. Sort dried raffia. Wipe with damp rag, pressing firmly between the fingers. Trim butt ends straight and tips slanting to make identification easy when working.
  2. Choose 24 leaves for check weaves on base. Place in pairs on top of each other. Arrange six pairs close to each other with a heavy weight on top.
  3. Lift alternate pairs and weave in a pair of leaves; tuck in as tightly as possible against weight. Weave in two more pairs then move weight onto woven leaves.
  4. Weave in other three pairs. Pack very tightly against other leaves to prevent gaps appearing when the leaves dry out. Remove the weight. Pull leaves at ends to flatten.
  5. Take long leaves. Double it around initial stake and begin pairing. Check weave to make spider, separating pair of leaves until all 24 are used singly.
  6. Continue pairing until base is just bigger than diameter of mold. Tie the leaves' base to the mold with two lengths of garden string at right angle.
  7. To join new weaver, overlap new leaves and hold overlapping pieces together. Work them as one as you continue weaving. Trim end later.
  8. After pairing for about 4 cm, add each weaver or three rods waling for one round. Build up sides with rows of pairing separated by a row of waling for appearance and strength.
  9. End with five rows of pairing before beginning border. Insert the football lacer up pairing to pick up and thread ends of pairing weavers into weaving to conceal them.
  10. To mend broken strand: Insert lacer up four rows of pairing, insert new leaves, and pull into place. Trim broken leaves. Trim end of new leaves when they are firmly woven in.
  11. To begin vale border: Working anti- clockwise, bend down each stake in turn diagonally behind the stake on its right. Work all round basket keeping bends equal.
  12. Second stage of border: Continue in an anti-clockwise direction and bend each stake under one on the right then turn it up diagonally. Keep work neat and even.
  13. Before finishing the border, cut the strings holding the basket to the mold; working on the next stage, carefully remove the basket.
  14. Finishing border: Insert lacer up four times of pairing. Thread in end of rush and pull through. Continue all around the basket until all the ends are neat.
  15. Mark positions of handles. Insert pair of leaves (butt to tip) three rows down. Pull through halfway. Insert second pair through next gap. Take two leaves in each hand.
  16. Twist right-hand pair four times. Change hands and twist the new right-hand pair four times. Coil both twists together. Twist and coil until the handle is of the correct length.
  17. Separate the end into two pairs. Carefully insert ends of each pair on opposite sides of rim. Pull through and weave ends away to make handle secure.
  18. Make the second handle in the same way. Ensure that it is of the same length. Secure firmly. Finally, trim any loose ends left protruding from the basket. Allow to dry in an airy place.
