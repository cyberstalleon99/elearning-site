Lesson

Identify and Use of Materials: Tools and Equipment for Basketry

Basketry Materials

The Philippines serves as a home to a wide variety of plants growing on forests, swamps, hills, or plains that are excellent materials for basketry. The plants that are usually used in basketry are generally classified into ferns, pandan, grasses, bamboos, sedges, the country, growing with other species of plants.

  1. Rattan core (also known as reed) is one of the most popular materials used in basketry because it is readily avail- able. It is pliable and very sturdy when it is woven correctly. Flat reed is used for most square baskets; oval reed is used for round baskets; and round reed is used for coiling baskets. Another advantage of using reed as material in basketry is that it can be dyed easily so it could look like an oak or hickory.

    Rattan core (also known as reed)

  2. Abaca is also used as a material in basketry because it is very flexible and durable. It is the strongest of the natural fibers and resistant to saltwater damage. Because of its good qualities, it is a sought-after material. It can be used to make handicrafts like bags, carpets, clothing, and furniture. The inner fibers of abaca are used in manufacturing hats, including the manila hats, hammocks, matting, cordage, ropes, coarse twines, and different types of canvas. The abaca is also known as manila hemp in the market although it is unlike true hemp. Other varieties are known as Cebu hemp and Davao hemp.

    Abaca

  3. Pandan is a tropical plant widely used in cooking as flavoring, but it could also be used as a material in basketry. Baskets made of pandan are used as a pot for cooking rice.

    Pandan

  4. Tikug belongs to the grass family. It is very versatile, pliable, and smooth. Because of its good characteristics, it has been a popular and excellent material used in weaving quality mats. Various colorful and functional handcrafted products can also be made from it.

    Tikug

  5. Buri palm is an excellent material for making coiled basket. Many industrial products are derived from buri palms. The young leaves of the plant are made into strips. Raffia is the skin stripped from the leaf segments of the buri shoots before the blade has unfolded.

    Buri palm

  6. Karagumoy leaves are coarse. They split while they are fresh, and they press as they dry. These strips are used for weaving mats, hats, bags, or work basket.

    Karagumoy

  7. Nito fern grows in humid, shaded places where timbers is not very thick. It loses many of its leaves as it grows. The stem of the nito changes as it grows from green to light green, to dark brown, and finally to black.

    Nito fern

  8. Dog rose, honeysuckle, and blackberry briars are examples of plants used once their spikes and creepers have been worn off.

    Dog rose, honeysuckle, and blackberry briars

  9. Willow grows fast and is easy to harvest. It is also known for its flexibility.

    Willow grows

  10. Water hyacinth has become a serious pest in some areas. For it to become useful, water hyacinth is made into one of the materials to make handicrafts.

    Water hyacinth

Basketry Tools

The main tools of basketry are simple, and these include the following:

  1. Awl or Boldkin is used for making holes for inserting willow, cane, or reed. Small bodkin is used for pegging the work to the workboard while medium bodkin is for easing cane into position and for tapping down the rows of weaving to keep the work level.

    Awl or Boldkin

  2. Secateurs are used for cutting off ends of a cane.

    Secateurs

  3. Nose pliers are used to pinch the reed where you want to bend it at right angle when making borders.

    Nose pliers

  4. Tape measure or Meter sticks is used for measuring materials.

    Tape measure

  5. Rapping iron looks like a chisel, but it is blunt and used for tapping down the willow weave into position.

    Rapping iron

  6. Utility knife is used for cutting materials, sharpening stakes, and trimming. It is originally a fixed blade knife with a cutting edge suitable for general work.

    Utility knife
