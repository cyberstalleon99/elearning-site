Kinds of Macramé Knots

Knots and weaving used in making crafts can be traced back to ancient times when people used these activities during their extra time. Macramé items look beautiful, but often too complicated and hard to do. You won't believe it, but the design of the most macramé works is built up from the different combinations of a few basic knots! There are few of them.

If you are a beginner and you want to learn macramé, it is very important to familiarize yourself with the different knots used to create beautiful designs. Below are the different types of macramé knots.

Square Knots

A square knot is one of the most widely used macramé knots and it can be created as left facing or right facing. These knots need to have at least four cords (two working cords and two filler cords) but can have more. The first and last cords are the working cords. We'll call them working cord one and four. The middle cords are filler cords and we'll number those two and three. These cords will switch places but will still keep their original numbering.



The Half Hitch Knots

The half hitch is a simple overhand knot where the working ends of a line is brought over and under the standing part. The vertical hitch makes a very dense work and usually is used for making belts, other accessories, and also rugs and wall decors.



Lark's Head Knot

The first knot you'll need to know is the Lark's Head Knot, sometimes referred to as a Cow Hitch Knots. This knot is what gets your macramé cords attached to an object, such as dowel, branch, or an anchor cord and usually used for garnishing the edges of macramé pieces.



Finishing Knot

This method is often used for finishing a macramé work, if you don't want to leave tassels.



Decorative Knots

Decorative, ornamental, or fancy knots are not necessarily strong but they have an aesthetic appeal. It usually exhibit repetitive patterns and are complex in structure. They were used back in the ancient times to enhance the ship- like shape of a nautical vessel.

Decorative knots can be made with single or multiple strands and it may be used alone or in conjunction with other knots of the same nature. The tying guides can be utilized for making it around poles, curtains, handles, and in designing jewelry. You can tie them with paracord, ribbon, rope ends, braid and lanyards.

Chinese Knots

A Chinese knot is a knot that is tied and woven from a single length of cord or rope to be a variety of shapes and varying complexity. Its shape has its own symbolic meaning, and nowadays you can find them as decoration, gifts for special occasions and adornment on clothes.

Most knots are double-layered and symmetrical and have two cords entering the knots from the top and two leaving from the bottom. The weavers can use variety of colors, but they usually weave deep red ones because they believe it signifies good fortune.



Celtic Knots

These knots are complete loops that have no start or finish and could be said to represent eternity whether this means loyalty, faith, friendship or love. Only one thread is used in each design which symbolizes how life and eternity are interconnected.
