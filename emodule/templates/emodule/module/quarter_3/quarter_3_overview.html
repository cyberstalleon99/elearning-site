Overview

Production is the process of creating goods and services. It requires a set of input to produce a set of output. Our country is blessed with abundant sources of raw materials in making basketry products.

This lesson focuses on the acquisition of knowledge and development of skills in the production of basketry products. Skills such as preparing a project proposal, computing for cost and the selling price, and formulating criteria to evaluate basketry products are all included and given emphasis.

It is hoped that this lesson will not only provide knowledge and skills needed in producing basketry products but also develop efficiency, creativity, and resourcefulness in the students.

Study these terms

  • Direct Labor - the workers whose output is closely related to or associated with the making of the product.
  • Direct Materials - raw materials used in production that become part of the product.
  • Markup - he margin of difference between the cost price and the selling price.
  • Manufacturing overhead - all other costs incurred in production, but do not become part of the product.
  • Price - the value placed on the goods and services offered to the public.
  • Production - the process of creating products and services that a manufacturer sells to its customers.
  • Project proposal - a detailed description of a series of activities aimed at solving a certain problem.
  • Raw materials - tangible physical resources needed in the creation of products which are used for production.
  • Resources - the material, manpower, and machine used in creating products and services.
