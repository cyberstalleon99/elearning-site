Project Proposal for Basketry Products

A project proposal also called a "statement of work" is defined as a detailed description of a series of activities aimed at solving a certain problem (NEBIU 2002). A project proposal is a prerequisite to achieve success in whatever project you plan to do. The first step in planning is choosing the project to be done. In deciding for any project, answer first the following questions:

  • Will I and other people appreciate this project?
  • Is this project beneficial?
  • Is this project economical?
  • Are the materials to be used available?
  • Can I follow the steps in making this project?

The following are the parts of a project proposal:

  1. Title - talks about the title of the project, team name and individual member names, date, and the picture of the project.
  2. Rationale - brief statement of the project, procedures employed to carry out the project, and the project outcome.
  3. Objectives - talks about the goal or purpose of the project.
  4. Design Drawing or Illustration - shows the design of the project.
  5. Project Organization and Management - tells how the project will be handled. This includes the task phases from planning to production, division of responsibilities, and duties of each team member.
  6. Budget - tells how much is to be spent for the whole project to finish.
Sample Project Proposal

Title: Production of Basket

Rationale:

Basketry refers to the art and craft of creating containers, usually in the form of baskets, by weaving flexible plant materials or other materials together. It is a technique that has been practiced by various cultures throughout history, utilizing natural materials such as grasses, reeds, branches, and bark strips. Basketry serves both functional and decorative purposes, providing a means to carry, store, and organize items, as well as serving as artistic expressions. The weaving techniques used in basketry can vary, including coiling, plaiting, twining, and stake and strand weaving, among others. Basketry is a versatile craft that encompasses a wide range of styles and designs, reflecting the cultural traditions and creativity of different societies.

This project gives the students an opportunity to discover a lot of things about basketry. As they go along creating things using basketry, they will also discover that it can be a great source of income, which does not require many supplies or tools.

  3. Apply one's aesthetic sense in making a basket.
  4. Show enthusiasm by working.
  5. Show creativity in promoting the product.

Product Costing and Selling Price

The product cost is made up of three parts, namely, direct materials, direct labor, and manufacturing overhead.

  1. Direct materials refer to the raw materials that become part of the product. For example, the milk and sugar used to make pastillas or ice cream; the leather used in making shoes; the precious stones used in crafting jewelry such as earrings and necklaces; or buri, tikug, and abaca in making baskets and food trays.
  2. Direct labor comprises the workers whose outputs are closely related to or directly related to the manufacturing of the merchandise. From the same example given, direct labor is provided by the worker who prepares and mixes the ingredients for the ice cream; the shoemaker for the shoes; the mounter who sets the stones on jewelry; or crafter for basketry.
  3. Manufacturing overhead includes all other costs incurred in production but do not become part of the product. These include:
    • the use of the facilities, machinery, and equipment and their maintenance.
    • the use of utilities, which includes power, water, and fuel used in running the plantation.
    • salaries for the employees or workers who are indirectly involved in making the product (e.g., the production foreman, the clerk, and security guard);
    • transportation costs of raw materials; and
    • office supplies and expenses such as paper, telephone, photocopying, etc. used in the operation of the plantation offices.

Manufacturing overhead is made up of the following:

  • Fixed expenses are expenditure that continues even if no units are produced. It includes equipment downgrading, taxes, interest on debts, and rent payments.
  • Variable expenses are expenditure that varies with the volume of units produced. The major components of variable costs are labor and materials. Variable expenses are inconsistent and not fixed. They also include the portion of the utilities that varies with volume. The difference between selling price and variable cost is contribution. There will be profit when total contribution exceeds total fixed cost.

Computing for Cost and Selling Price

Price setting seems to be an easy thing to do; yet it is one of the most crucial aspects of marketing. Price is the value placed on goods and services offered to the public.

The basic method in setting the price of your product is cost-oriented pricing. Under this method, all costs are added and divided by the number of products produced in order to arrive at the selling price of a single product or unit. Markup refers to the margin of difference between the cost price and the selling price.

For example:

  1. List down all the costs of materials that were used in making your product.
  2. List down the operational expenses like labor cost, transportation, electricity, etc.
  3. Add all expenses in steps 1 and 2. This is your total cost.
  4. Multiply the sum in step 3 by .40 if the desired profit is 40%. This is your profit.
  5. Add the product of step 4 to the sum of step 3. This is your selling price.

  • Operational Cost:

Suggested Scoring Rubrics

Evaluation of a finished product is important in assessing one's learning. It will tell whether your outputs are excellent, satisfactory, or poor. It also gives you feedback on the aspects you need to improve in order to produce better work next time.

Evaluation is done in many ways. The use of rubric is a helpful tool to use in evaluating student progress and learning after each lesson.

Evaluation using a rubric can be done by you, a classmate, or your teacher. The more people are involved in the evaluation process, the more accurate and reliable the result will be.
