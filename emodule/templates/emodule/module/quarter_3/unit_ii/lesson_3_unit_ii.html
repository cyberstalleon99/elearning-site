Production Process

There are four stages of production involved in the production process, namely, operation, assembly, finishing, and inspection.

  1. Operation involves a series of steps a raw material goes through in the creation of a product.
  2. Assembly
    3. is putting all parts together to form a final product.
  3. Finishing
    4. is a series of activities involved in the final polishing of a product such as varnishing, trimming, cleaning, etc
  4. Inspection
    5. involves the process undertaken to make sure that the operation has been carried out correctly as to the specified quality and quantity of the product.

The resources needed in the production of macramé and basketry products are the following:

  1. Material resources. These are tangible, physical resources needed in the creation of macramé and basketry products which are used for production. Examples are raw materials like abaca, buri, tikug, and pandan, and tools used in making products like macramé board, thread burner, scissors, tweezers, and hand knives, among others.
  2. Human resources. These are the most important resources. These include the people who plan and create products. Human resources development could be achieved through education and training.
  3. Financial resources. These involve funds for various purposes.
  4. Informational resources. Correct and complete information is vital to the success of producing products. Facts are very important in making plans, decisions, and implementation of plans. One must know what, when, where, and how of producing the products and the recent technological developments.
